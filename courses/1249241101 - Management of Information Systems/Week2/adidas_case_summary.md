## The Adidas Case: Key Facts

### Company Overview
- **Industry:** Leading global maker of athletic shoes, clothing, and accessories
- **Scale:** Sells 1.2 million pairs of shoes daily (p. 387)
- **Headquarters:** Herzogenaurach, Germany (p. 387)
- **Employees:** Over 57,000 worldwide (p. 387)
- **Revenue:** Net sales of 23.6 billion Euros (US $27 billion) in 2019 (p. 387)
- **Market Position:** Second largest sportswear manufacturer in the world after Nike (p. 387)

### Digital Transformation
- Adidas is a leader in digital and online marketing (p. 387)
- The company's most important store is no longer physical—it's the website (p. 387)
- E-commerce is Adidas's most profitable point-of-sale channel (p. 387)
- Online sales reached 5.509 billion Euros (US $6.180 billion) in 2019 (p. 387)

## Question 2: Adidas's Business Strategy and CRM's Role

### Business Strategy
1. **Customer-Centric Differentiation:** Adidas does not compete on price but on the quality of its brand and customer experience.

2. **Digital-First Approach:** Transition from brick-and-mortar to digital as the preferred shopping medium.

3. **Personalized Customer Relationships:** The company wants a relationship with customers that is more personal than competitors like Amazon.

4. **Omnichannel Experience:** Providing connected and personalized customer experiences across all channels (website, mobile, email, social media, telephone).

### Role of CRM in Strategy
- **Central to Business:** Customers are clearly at the center of Adidas's business.
- **Competitive Differentiation:** CRM helps differentiate Adidas from competitors and leads to increased sales.
- **Individual Customer Knowledge:** CRM allows Adidas to answer: "Who is this person? How much do we know about that individual? Where did that person come from? What is that person interested in?
- **One-to-One Connection:** Adidas can now connect one-to-one with every customer, across multiple channels and on any device.

**Quote from Jacqueline Smith-Dubendorfer, Adidas Vice President of Digital Experience Design:**
> "Using Salesforce for customer relationship management has enhanced the company's ability to treat each customer as an individual."

## Question 3: How Information Systems Support Adidas's Strategy

### Salesforce CRM Platform Components

1. **Salesforce.com CRM Tools** (p. 387)
   - Identify key customer segments
   - Develop closer ties to customers
   - Design differentiated experiences tailored to each customer's needs
   - Provide single view of each customer across all channels

2. **Salesforce Marketing Cloud** (p. 387)
   - Create and manage marketing relationships and campaigns
   - Integrated solutions for:
     - Customer journey management
     - Email marketing
     - Mobile marketing
     - Social media
     - Web personalization
     - Advertising
     - Content creation
     - Content management
     - Data analysis
   - **Journey Builder:** Helps marketers tailor campaigns to customers' behavior, needs, demographics, and communication channel preferences
   - Predictive analytics for deciding optimal channel for messages

3. **Sales Cloud and Service Cloud** (p. 387)
   - Unified customer experience
   - Prevents customers from being contacted separately by representatives from sales, marketing, and service groups
   - **Service Cloud capabilities:**
     - Automate service processes
     - Streamline workflows
     - Locate key articles, topics, and experts
     - Information for 1,100 customer service agents
     - "Listen" and respond to customers across social platforms
     - Automatically route cases to appropriate agents
   - **Service for Apps:** Embed customer support software into mobile applications (live agent video chat, screen sharing, on-screen guided assistance)

4. **Commerce Cloud** (p. 388)
   - Centralized e-commerce site deployed globally
   - Supports every language and currency for worldwide business
   - Uses customer knowledge and preferences to create better products
   - Products can be manufactured and delivered very rapidly

5. **Salesforce DMP (Data Management Platform)** (p. 388)
   - Captures online and web behavior from digital actions across all channels and devices

6. **Adidas Mobile App** (p. 388)
   - Customizes content, interactions, and products based on customer's personal preferences and behavior
   - Features include:
     - Customized product recommendations
     - Color preference and location tracking
     - Order tracking
     - Blog posts and personalized articles
     - Videos and real-time updates on sports stars
     - **Einstein:** Intelligent online chat tool for customer questions and customized product recommendations
   - Works with Apple Pay and Android Pay mobile payment systems

## Question 4: How Salesforce Made Adidas More Competitive

### Competitive Advantages Gained

1. **Personalization at Scale** (p. 387-388)
   - Deliver service that is more personalized and convenient
   - Whatever form each customer prefers—email, web, social media, or telephone—all from a single application

2. **Global E-commerce Capability** (p. 388)
   - Deploy centralized e-commerce site globally using Commerce Cloud
   - Support every language and currency required for company's business throughout the globe

3. **Faster Product Development and Delivery** (p. 388)
   - Use knowledge of individual customers and their preferences to create better products
   - Products can be manufactured and delivered to customers very rapidly

4. **Deeper Customer Understanding** (p. 388)
   - CRM provides information for Adidas to adapt what it presents to customers to deliver as close as it can to what customers are actually looking for

5. **Unified Customer View** (p. 387)
   - Single view of each customer across all channels through which that person interacts with the company

### Business Changes

1. **Shift from Physical to Digital** (p. 387)
   - Website became most important "store"
   - E-commerce became most profitable channel

2. **Customer Service Transformation** (p. 387)
   - 1,100 customer service agents now have information to help customers
   - Service Cloud can "listen" and respond to customers across social platforms
   - Automatic routing of cases to appropriate agents

3. **Data-Driven Marketing** (p. 387)
   - Predictive analytics to determine best channels for marketing messages
   - Journey Builder for tailored campaigns

## Supporting Concepts from Chapter 9

### What is CRM? (p. 381-382)
- CRM systems capture and integrate customer data from all over the organization
- Consolidate and analyze the data
- Distribute results to various systems and customer touch points across the enterprise
- **Touch point:** Method of interaction with customer (telephone, email, customer service desk, website, wireless device, retail store)

### Business Value of CRM Systems (p. 386)
- Increased customer satisfaction
- Reduced direct-marketing costs
- More effective marketing
- Lower costs for customer acquisition and retention
- Increased sales revenue by identifying most profitable customers for focused marketing and cross-selling

### Key CRM Concepts
- **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV):** Relationship between revenue from a specific customer, expenses incurred in acquiring and servicing that customer, and expected life of relationship (p. 386)
- **Churn Rate:** Measures the number of customers who stop using or purchasing products/services from a company (p. 386)
- **Cross-selling:** Marketing of complementary products to customers (p. 383)

### Operational vs. Analytical CRM (p. 385)
- **Operational CRM:** Customer-facing applications such as sales force automation, call center, and customer service support
- **Analytical CRM:** Applications that analyze customer data generated by operational CRM to provide information for improving business performance
